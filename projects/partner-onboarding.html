IBM SkillsBuild Partner Onboarding

Structured enablement program for 30+ nonprofit and academic partners

Overview

Designed the end-to-end partner onboarding program for IBM SkillsBuild, creating a repeatable process that brings new nonprofit and academic partners from initial agreement to full platform activation. The program serves 30+ partners and was built to scale across different partner types, learner populations, and implementation contexts.

Program Components

Pre-Implementation Kickoff

Structured kickoff template covering partner goals, target learner demographics, technical requirements, timeline milestones, and success metrics. Ensures alignment before any platform configuration begins.

Template Alignment

Enablement Sessions

Two tailored session agendas — one for Adult tenant partners (workforce development, career changers) and one for Student tenant partners (academic institutions, youth programs). Each covers platform navigation, content curation, and reporting.

Adult Tenant Student Tenant

Permissions Framework

Role-based permissions matrix defining access levels for partner admins, instructors, and learners. Balances partner autonomy with platform governance and data privacy requirements.

RBAC Governance

Kickoff Meeting Structure

The pre-implementation kickoff follows a structured agenda designed to surface critical information early:

  1. Welcome & Introductions — Team introductions, roles, and communication preferences
  2. Partner Goals & Context — Organizational mission, target population, existing programs, and success definition
  3. Platform Overview — IBM SkillsBuild capabilities, content library, and customization options
  4. Technical Setup — SSO integration, branding requirements, LMS connections, and data flows
  5. Content Strategy — Learning pathway selection, custom content needs, and localization requirements
  6. Timeline & Milestones — Phased rollout plan with go-live date, pilot group, and scaling targets
  7. Success Metrics — KPIs, reporting cadence, and quarterly review schedule
  8. Next Steps — Action items with owners and deadlines

Enablement Session Design

Adult Tenant Session

Focused on workforce development use cases — career changers, upskilling programs, and professional development:

  • Platform navigation and admin dashboard walkthrough
  • Creating and assigning learning pathways for adult learners
  • Badge and credential management
  • Reporting and learner progress tracking
  • Integration with existing workforce programs
  • Best practices for learner engagement and retention

Student Tenant Session

Tailored for academic institutions and youth-serving organizations:

  • Classroom integration strategies and LMS connectivity
  • Age-appropriate content curation and pathway design
  • Instructor tools and progress monitoring
  • Student self-service features and portfolio building
  • Co-curricular and extracurricular program design
  • Reporting for institutional stakeholders and accreditation
