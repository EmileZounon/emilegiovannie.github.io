DSM Onboarding Plan

Getting new Digital Success Managers operational in 10 business days

Overview

This two-week structured onboarding plan was designed to bring new Digital Success Managers (DSMs) at IBM SkillsBuild from day-one orientation to full operational readiness. The plan covers platform training, buddy shadowing, reporting techniques, partner meetings, and personal goal-setting — organized as a day-by-day schedule across 10 business days.

Week 1 — Foundation

The first week focuses on understanding the role, the platform, and the partner ecosystem.

Day 1–2

Orientation & Context

Team introductions, org chart overview, IBM SkillsBuild mission and strategy briefing. Review of DSM role expectations, KPIs, and success metrics. Access setup for all tools and platforms.

Day 3–4

Platform Deep Dive

Hands-on IBM SkillsBuild platform training. Admin dashboard navigation, content library exploration, learning pathway creation, and badge management. Practice with sandbox environment.

Day 5

Buddy Shadowing

Shadow an experienced DSM through a full day: partner check-in calls, content curation workflow, escalation handling, and weekly reporting routine. Debrief with buddy at end of day.

Week 2 — Application

The second week transitions from learning to doing, with increasing independence.

Day 6–7

Reporting & Analytics

Training on partner reporting tools and dashboards. Practice pulling learner engagement data, completion rates, and pathway adoption metrics. Create a sample partner report from real data.

Day 8

Partner Meeting Practice

Attend a live partner meeting with mentor DSM. Afterwards, conduct a mock partner check-in with role-play scenarios covering common situations: onboarding questions, content requests, escalations.

Day 9

Independent Partner Work

Lead a partner check-in call with mentor observing. Handle content curation requests independently. Complete first full weekly report for assigned partners.

Day 10

Goal Setting & Review

30-60-90 day goal setting with manager. Review first two weeks, identify knowledge gaps, and create a personal development plan. Officially transition to managing assigned partner accounts.

Design Principles

  • Progressive complexity — Each day builds on the previous, moving from observation to participation to independence
  • Buddy system — Pairing with an experienced DSM ensures tacit knowledge transfer that documentation alone can't provide
  • Practice before performance — Mock scenarios and sandbox environments before real partner interactions
  • Structured reflection — Daily debrief questions and end-of-week check-ins with manager
  • Goal ownership — The 30-60-90 day plan is co-created by the new DSM, not imposed top-down

Resources Included

Day-by-Day Schedule

Detailed agenda for each of the 10 business days with specific activities, time blocks, and learning objectives.

Platform Cheat Sheet

Quick-reference guide for common SkillsBuild admin tasks: creating pathways, managing users, pulling reports.

Partner Meeting Template

Standardized agenda and notes template for weekly partner check-ins, ensuring consistency across all DSMs.

30-60-90 Day Template

Goal-setting framework with example milestones for partner portfolio growth, platform mastery, and strategic initiatives.

Impact

This onboarding plan standardized the DSM ramp-up process, ensuring that every new team member reaches operational readiness within two weeks regardless of their prior experience with the platform. It reduced the informal, ad-hoc onboarding that previously led to inconsistent partner experiences and extended ramp-up periods.
